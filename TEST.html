Arguably the most storied rivalry in football is between the Chicago Bears and Green Bay Packers. These two franchises are the oldest in the entire league and have competed against each other for over 100 seasons. Beginning in the year 1921, this matchup is the leagueâ€™s most played with each team running into each other 202 times across the regular season and playoffs. Both teams have combined for 65 players in the Pro Football Hall of Fame, 22 NFL Championships, and 5 Super Bowls. An average football fan would assume this is the most continuous rivalry in the history of football. Nevertheless, that distinction belongs to the Green Bay Packers and Detroit Lions. Detroit and Green Bay have played twice each season since 1932. However, due to a 1982 NFL Players Association strike, Chicago and Green Bay did not play either matchup that season. Green Bay took the overall series lead in 2017, and now lead all-time 101-95-6.
